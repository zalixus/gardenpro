Frequently Asked Questions

What services does GardenPro offer? +
GardenPro specialises in landscape design, garden design & setup, garden maintenance, sourcing exotic fruits, custom terrarium builds, aqua terrariums, and our upcoming green co-working space — Temple Tree.
Does GardenPro design gardens for homes and offices? +
Yes! We design and set up gardens for residential homes, corporate offices, resorts, educational institutions, and commercial properties across India. Each project is customised to meet your specific needs and environment.
Where is GardenPro located? +
We're based in Bengaluru, Karnataka, but we work pan-India. We've successfully delivered projects across numerous states and continue to expand our reach to serve more of India.
How long does a typical garden design project take? +
Project timelines vary based on complexity, size, and scope. A small residential garden typically takes 4–8 weeks from concept to installation, while larger landscape projects may take 3–6 months. We provide detailed timelines during the consultation phase.
What is Temple Tree? +
Temple Tree is GardenPro's upcoming green co-working space — a nature-immersed workspace designed for deep focus, creativity, and human well-being. It combines flexible co-working amenities with curated gardens, natural light, and a philosophy that work and wellness go hand-in-hand.
Does GardenPro provide ongoing maintenance? +
Absolutely. We offer tailored maintenance plans for all projects — from weekly pruning and watering to seasonal care, pest management, and fertilising. Most clients opt for our monthly or seasonal maintenance programs to keep their gardens thriving year-round.
Can GardenPro help with exotic fruit plants? +
Yes! We specialise in sourcing and supplying rare and tropical fruit plants across India. Whether you're looking for dragon fruit, rambutan, sapodilla, custard apple, or other exotic varieties, we can source them and provide guidance on cultivation.
What's the difference between an open and closed terrarium? +
Open terrariums are exposed to air and suit drought-tolerant plants like succulents — they require periodic watering. Closed terrariums are sealed glass containers that create a self-sustaining ecosystem with higher humidity — perfect for tropical plants and moisture-loving species. We recommend based on your space and preferences.
